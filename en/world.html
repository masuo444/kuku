What is KUKU

KUKU is
a new species born between the tree god "Kukunochi" and humans‚Äî
the so-called "Woodfolk."

Parts of their bodies are made of wood,
and they can sustain life and heal wounds by absorbing light.

Strong like trees,
gentle like humans,
beings destined to live with the forest.

Some KUKU house spirits in wooden boxes called "Masu" that they wear on their chests,
borrowing their power for battle and healing.
This is called "Spirit Art" and represents the symbolic power of KUKU.

Why Kukunochi mingled with humans and gave birth to KUKU
remains shrouded in mystery.

Masu

The symbolic equipment that KUKU carry.
Originally said to be an "amulet" given by the god Kukunochi to their children.

Inside the Masu sleeps a spirit contracted by each KUKU,
awakening through acts of battle, prayer, music, poetry, and dance.

A KUKU's Masu has been with them since birth
and changes its wood grain along with their heart.
When a spirit dwells within, the Masu glows,
serving as protection in battle, a beacon in rituals,
and even an instrument that resonates with music in art.

Spirits

In KUKU's world, spirits dwell in all natural phenomena.
They are energy entities with diverse attributes such as "light," "wind," "water," "fire," "earth," "flower," and "shadow."

By contracting with spirits, KUKU can "borrow" their power,
but this is not domination‚Äîit's a relationship of resonance.
If the heart becomes clouded, the spirit falls silent; if the heart is pure, they sing together.

"Spirits are the words of nature. Only those who listen know their voice."

Spirits dwell in the "Masu" and grow alongside their masters.
Over long years, the Masu breathes as if alive,
and when the spirit and KUKU's heart become one, unknown powers are unleashed.

The Tree Village and Root City

A settlement deep in the forest where many KUKU live.

It is formed by the intertwining roots and branches of trees.

At the center stands the World Tree, said to have been created by the tree god Kukunochi, with the settlement surrounding it.
From part of the World Tree's trunk extends an underground district of roots. This is called "Root City" and serves as the center of trade and culture.

Crossing wooden bridges that stretch along tree trunks,
you'll find wooden workshops, small teahouses, pharmacies using forest fruits,
and craftsmen's workshops where Masu are polished.

The existence of the Tree Village above ground and Root City below is the reason KUKU have prospered in the forest world for thousands of years.
